1. Furnace Won’t Turn On: Common Causes and Fixes

If your furnace is not turning on, it can leave you in the cold, and there are several potential reasons for this:

  • Thermostat Issues: The thermostat may be set incorrectly or malfunctioning, preventing the furnace from activating.
  • Tripped Circuit Breaker: A tripped breaker can prevent the furnace from receiving power.
  • Blown Fuse or Faulty Wiring: Electrical issues can disrupt power to the furnace.
  • Pilot Light or Ignition Failure: For gas furnaces, if the pilot light or ignition system fails, the furnace won’t start.

ALP Heating’s Fix: ALP Heating technicians will check the thermostat, reset the circuit breaker, and replace fuses or repair wiring as needed. They can also relight the pilot light or replace the ignition system to restore proper operation.

2. Furnace Blowing Cold Air: Why Isn’t It Heating?

If your furnace is blowing cold air, you may have one of these issues:

  • Thermostat Set to "Fan Only": If the thermostat is set to "fan," it will blow air but not warm it.
  • Dirty Air Filter: A clogged filter can block airflow, causing the furnace to overheat and blow cold air.
  • Ignition or Pilot Light Problems: A faulty ignition or pilot light can prevent the furnace from producing heat.
  • Gas Supply Issues: If there’s no gas, the furnace can’t generate heat.

ALP Heating’s Fix: ALP Heating will adjust the thermostat settings, replace the air filter, and inspect the ignition system. They will also check the gas lines to ensure smooth operation.

3. Furnace Making Strange Noises: What Does It Mean?

If your furnace is making strange noises, it could signal a problem:

  • Banging or Popping: Caused by a delayed ignition or air trapped in the ductwork.
  • Rattling or Screeching: Caused by loose parts or a worn blower motor.
  • Whistling or Hissing: Often due to restricted airflow, caused by a clogged air filter or duct leaks.

ALP Heating’s Fix: ALP Heating will inspect the furnace to clean or repair the burner assembly, tighten loose parts, lubricate the blower motor, and replace filters as necessary. They will also check ducts for leaks.

4. Furnace Cycling On and Off: Solving the Short-Cycling Issue

If your furnace turns on and off frequently, it may be short-cycling, which can be caused by:

  • Dirty Air Filter: A clogged filter can overheat the furnace and cause it to shut down.
  • Faulty Thermostat: An incorrect or malfunctioning thermostat may cause unnecessary cycling.
  • Limit Switch Issues: A faulty limit switch can cause the furnace to shut off before completing its cycle.

ALP Heating’s Fix: ALP Heating will replace the air filter, recalibrate or replace the thermostat, and inspect the limit switch for any issues. If necessary, they will replace faulty components to ensure smooth operation.

5. Furnace Not Producing Enough Heat: Insufficient Heating Solutions

If your furnace is running but not producing enough heat, it could be due to:

  • Incorrect Thermostat Settings: The thermostat may be set too low or improperly configured.
  • Dirty Air Filter: A clogged filter can prevent proper airflow and reduce heat output.
  • Leaky Ducts: Duct leaks can cause heated air to escape before it reaches your rooms.
  • Faulty Burner or Flame Sensor: These components may need to be cleaned or replaced to restore full heating power.

6. Furnace Leaking Water: Causes and Solutions

If your furnace is leaking water, it’s often due to problems with the condensate system:

  • Clogged Condensate Drain Line: Dirt or debris can block the drain and cause water buildup.
  • Faulty Condensate Pump: A malfunctioning pump can cause water to leak around the furnace.
  • Damaged Heat Exchanger: A damaged heat exchanger may result in water leakage.
